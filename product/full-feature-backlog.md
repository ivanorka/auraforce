# Full Feature Backlog

This maps Lewis's full feature list into a phased build plan.

The rule is simple: the first version must prove revenue, not contain every module.

## Phase 1 - Real Estate MVP

Build first:

- Basic CRM
- lead capture
- chatbots / WhatsApp / email
- unified inbox
- appointment booking system
- calendar
- AI receptionist
- AI sales assistant
- simple sales automation
- basic business intelligence / stats
- admin panel
- usage tracking
- wallet / top-up logic

Reason:

This solves the first commercial promise: leads arrive, AI answers, qualifies, books, follows up and gives the owner visibility.

## Phase 2 - Strong SaaS Platform

Add after MVP validation:

- multi-tenancy hardening
- billing and subscriptions
- document management
- better sales automation
- reporting
- knowledge bases
- workflow automation
- client onboarding
- reputation management
- AI meeting assistant
- inbound call handling, if pilot demand justifies it

Reason:

This makes the product more commercially mature without turning it into a giant unfocused platform.

## Phase 3 - AI Employees And Industry Expansion

Add:

- AI employee marketplace
- AI Recruitment Assistant
- AI Customer Support Agent
- Real Estate Template
- Recruitment Template
- Car Rental Template
- law firm template
- clinic template
- document signing integrations
- new hire training plans / onboarding system

Reason:

Once the core workflow and usage economics are proven, these become packaged vertical expansions.

## Phase 4 - Larger Platform

Delay until there is strong proof or a strategic client pays for it:

- cold calling
- full AI call centre
- vehicle tracking
- HR / staff management
- marketing suite
- advanced invoicing
- website builder
- native mobile apps
- advanced business intelligence
- enterprise security features

Reason:

These features can be valuable, but they increase complexity, compliance needs, support load and build cost.
