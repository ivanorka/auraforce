# Investor Q&A

## Why is this not just another CRM?

A CRM stores data. AuraForce AI executes the front line of the process: instant response, lead qualification, booking, follow-up, routing and AI-human handoff. The CRM is one control layer inside the broader AI command platform.

## Why start with real estate?

Real estate has high-value leads, urgent response windows, heavy WhatsApp usage and clear appointment workflows. That makes ROI easier to prove than in a vague horizontal launch.

## What is the moat?

The moat is not a single model. It is the operational packaging: vertical workflows, scripts, role-based AI employees, permissions, usage wallet, onboarding, reporting and domain-specific process knowledge.

## How do you control AI costs?

AI usage is metered through token credits, model routing, voice minutes, WhatsApp/SMS/email volume, storage and top-ups. Plans should include usage caps and margin, not unlimited AI.

## What happens when AI gets something wrong?

AI employees need permissions, scripts, escalation rules, human handoff, audit logs and clear limits. AuraForce should automate repetitive execution while keeping humans in control of high-value decisions.

## Why will agencies pay?

Because missed leads and slow follow-up have direct economic cost. The product should prove value through response time, qualified leads, booked viewings, follow-up completion and recovered inquiries.

## Why Dubai?

Dubai is aligned with AI adoption, premium service expectations, fast real estate activity and WhatsApp-first commercial behavior. The brand can credibly position as high-trust Dubai luxury-tech.

## Why raise `$350k-$500k`?

It is enough to build the real estate MVP, support early AI/infra costs, run pilots and create commercial proof before a larger platform round.

A lean MVP can potentially be built for less, especially with offshore execution and heavy AI-assisted development. The reason to raise more is to avoid ending with only a demo: the company also needs pilot onboarding, usage buffer, integrations, legal/compliance, design, sales work and iteration runway.

## What do investors need to believe?

They need to believe the first wedge can produce paid demand and that the platform core can be reused across other verticals.

## What is the biggest risk?

Overbuilding before proving the first use case. The strategy should stay narrow until real estate pilots validate the product, pricing and workflow.

## Can the AI improve over time?

Yes, but it should be controlled. AI can suggest improvements based on reviewed conversations, lead outcomes, scripts and user feedback. Humans approve changes before they become part of the company knowledge base.

## Should AuraForce start with a native mobile app?

No, unless a pilot client creates a strong commercial reason. Start with a responsive web app and possibly PWA. Native iOS and Android apps should come after the core workflows are proven.
